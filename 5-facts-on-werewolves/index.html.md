# 5 Myth\-Busting Facts About Werewolves Every Fan Should Know

When a werewolf is involved, things can be pretty hairy—pun intended! We all know the classic tropes, but there's so much more to these legendary creatures. While I was scared of them as a child, I now have a deep appreciation for their lore and enjoy looking at depictions of them in modern media. I have never met a werewolf in real life, and I’m quite thankful for that, as I probably wouldn’t live to tell the tale (unless I had [Bella Swan's](https://twilight.allwomenstalk.com/5-signs-youre-in-love-with-edward/) luck). Right now, I am going to share 5 fascinating facts on werewolves that might surprise you.

## 5. Silver Won’t Kill

Believe what you want, but many folklore experts suggest that a silver bullet isn't the guaranteed end-all we see in movies. While it may cause significant discomfort and pain, it won’t necessarily cause a werewolf to burst into flames or vanish. According to [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/werewolf), the silver vulnerability is a relatively modern addition to the mythos.

## 4. Turning into One

The legends vary, but the most common ways to become a werewolf include:

- Being cursed by a powerful entity
- Being born into a werewolf lineage
- Being bitten by an existing werewolf
- Gaining the power through ancient sorcery

## 3. Telepathy

In many modern interpretations, werewolves communicate through telepathy. This mental link allows them to coordinate as a pack, making them incredibly effective hunters. Your chances of hearing them speak in a human language while in their wolf form are incredibly slim. If you're interested in other cinematic creatures, check out our list of [scary monsters from movies](https://twilight.allwomenstalk.com/5-scary-monsters-from-movies/).

## 2. Full Moon

Contrary to popular belief, a werewolf does not always have to wait until a full moon to reveal their true nature. In many traditions, they can shift at will or under intense emotional stress. You can track the current lunar cycles at [The Old Farmer's Almanac](https://www.almanac.com/astronomy/moon/calendar) to see when the next peak might occur!

## 1. Spotting a Werewolf

There are various physical and behavioral symptoms associated with werewolves in folklore. Here is a quick reference table for some of the most common signs:

| Symptom Type | Common Signs |
| :--- | :--- |
| Physical | Excessive hair, eyebrows meeting in the middle, long third finger |
| Behavioral | Uncontrollable anger, insomnia, difficulty pronouncing "W" words |
| Mystical | Magical marks on the palms of the hands |
